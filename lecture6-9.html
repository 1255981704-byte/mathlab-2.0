09 // FUNDAMENTAL THEOREM
DIFFERENTIATION ↔ INTEGRATION  •  DEFINITE INTEGRALS
ACCUMULATION AND RATE ARE INVERSE IDEAS
CONNECTION

From Accumulation to Rate

ACCUMULATION FUNCTION

For a continuous function \(f\), define the accumulated signed area from a fixed point \(a\) to a moving endpoint \(x\):

DEFINITION
\[\displaystyle F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,dt.\]

The letter \(t\) is a dummy variable. The output \(F(x)\) changes as the endpoint \(x\) moves.

Area above the axis adds; area below the axis subtracts.

GEOMETRIC PICTURE
NET ACCUMULATION // A TO X
THE BIG IDEA
A tiny increase \(dx\) adds approximately \(f(x)\,dx\) of area.
\[\displaystyle \frac{\text{change in area}}{\text{change in }x}\approx f(x).\]
THEOREM

FTC Part 1: Differentiate Accumulation

STATEMENT

If \(f\) is continuous, then differentiation recovers the original integrand:

FTC PART 1
\[\displaystyle \boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\int_a^x f(t)\,dt=f(x)}\]

For a composite upper endpoint \(g(x)\), use the chain rule:

FTC + CHAIN RULE
\[\displaystyle \boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\int_a^{g(x)}f(t)\,dt=f(g(x))g'(x)}\]
INTERPRETATION & VIDEO
● RECEXPAND
▶
THEOREM VIDEO // FTC 1
READ IT AS A PROCESS
1. Replace the dummy variable with the upper limit.
2. Multiply by the derivative of that upper limit.
3. Do not search for an antiderivative.

GIVEN
\[\displaystyle H(x)=\int_1^{x^3}\cos(t^2)\,dt\]
INTEGRAND\(f(t)=\cos(t^2)\)
UPPER LIMIT\(g(x)=x^3\)

WORKED SOLUTION
Insert \(x^3\) wherever the integrand has \(t\):
\[\displaystyle f(g(x))=\cos((x^3)^2)=\cos(x^6).\]
Multiply by \(g'(x)=3x^2\):
\[\displaystyle H'(x)=3x^2\cos(x^6).\]

UPPER MINUS LOWER
\[\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\int_{u(x)}^{v(x)}f(t)\,dt=f(v(x))v'(x)-f(u(x))u'(x)\]

THEOREM

FTC Part 2: Evaluate Definite Integrals

EVALUATION THEOREM

If \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and \(F'(x)=f(x)\), then

FTC PART 2
\[\displaystyle \boxed{\int_a^b f(x)\,dx=F(b)-F(a)}\]
EVALUATION NOTATION
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)\,dx=\left[F(x)\right]_a^b\]

A definite integral is a number, so the final answer does not include (+C).

Evaluate the definite integral.
INTEGRAL
\[\displaystyle \int_1^3\left(2x+\frac1x\right)dx\]
POWER RULE\(\int 2x\,dx=x^2\)
LOG RULE\(\int \frac1x\,dx=\ln|x|\)
SOLUTION & VIDEO
● RECEXPAND
▶
SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 03
WORKED SOLUTION
\[\displaystyle \int_1^3\left(2x+\frac1x\right)dx=\left[x^2+\ln|x|\right]_1^3\]
\[\displaystyle =(9+\ln3)-(1+\ln1)=8+\ln3.\]

PROPERTIES

Definite Integral Algebra

ORIENTATION & LINEARITY
ZERO WIDTH
\[\displaystyle \int_a^a f(x)\,dx=0\]
REVERSE LIMITS
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)\,dx=-\int_b^a f(x)\,dx\]
LINEARITY
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b(kf\pm g)=k\int_a^b f\pm\int_a^b g\]
ADD INTERVALS

A shared endpoint allows adjacent intervals to combine:

ADDITIVITY
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)\,dx+\int_b^c f(x)\,dx=\int_a^c f(x)\,dx\]

If \(\int_1^4 f=7\) and \(\int_4^6 f=-2\), then

QUICK APPLICATION
\[\displaystyle \int_1^6f(x)\,dx=7+(-2)=5.\]

Match orientation before combining intervals.

Net Area versus Total Area

PROBLEM & GRAPH
For \(f(x)=x-1\) on \([-1,2]\), find the net signed area and the total geometric area.
GRAPH // Y = X - 1
SOLUTION & VIDEO
● RECEXPAND
▶
SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 04
TWO DIFFERENT QUESTIONS
Net signed area:
\[\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{2}(x-1)\,dx=-\frac12.\]
Split at \(x=1\) and make both regions positive:
\[\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{2}|x-1|\,dx=2+\frac12=\frac52.\]
