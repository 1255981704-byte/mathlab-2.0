01

Right Riemann Sum

PROBLEM & GRAPH
Approximate \(\displaystyle\int_0^2x^2\,dx\) using \(n=4\) right-endpoint rectangles.
REFERENCE GRAPH // \(f(x)=x^2\)
SOLUTION & VIDEO
● REC EXPAND
▶
SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 01
DERIVATION
Four equal subintervals on \([0,2]\) give \(\Delta x=(2-0)/4=0.5\).
Right endpoints: \(0.5,\ 1,\ 1.5,\ 2\).
\[\displaystyle R_4=\sum_{i=1}^{4}f(x_i)\Delta x=0.5\,[f(0.5)+f(1)+f(1.5)+f(2)]\]
\[\displaystyle R_4=0.5(0.25+1+2.25+4)=3.75\]

RIEMANN SUM TO INTEGRAL
LIMIT IDEA
A rectangle contributes \(f(x_i^*)\Delta x_i\).
Adding all rectangles gives \(\sum f(x_i^*)\Delta x_i\).
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)\,dx=\lim_{\max\Delta x_i\to0}\sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i^*)\,\Delta x_i\]

Integral to Right Riemann Sum

PROBLEM
Write \(\displaystyle\int_0^\pi\sin x\,dx\) as a limit of right Riemann sums.
SOLUTION & VIDEO
● REC EXPAND
▶
SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 04
DERIVATION
Here \(a=0,\ b=\pi,\ \Delta x=\frac{\pi}{n}\).
Right endpoint of the \(i\)th subinterval: \(x_i=0+i\Delta x=\frac{i\pi}{n}\).
\[\displaystyle \int_0^\pi\sin x\,dx =\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n \sin\left(\frac{i\pi}{n}\right)\frac{\pi}{n} \]
