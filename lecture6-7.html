FORMULA

Integration by Parts Formula

STATEMENT

Start with \(d(uv)=u\,dv+v\,du\). Rearrange after integrating.

INDEFINITE FORM
\[\displaystyle \int u\,dv=uv-\int v\,du\]
DEFINITE FORM
\[\displaystyle \int_a^b u\,dv=[uv]_a^b-\int_a^b v\,du\]

The formula is short. The choice of \(u\) and \(dv\) is the whole game.

DERIVATION & VIDEO
● REC EXPAND
▶
DERIVATION VIDEO
DERIVATION
Product rule: \(\displaystyle d(uv)=u\,dv+v\,du\).
\[\displaystyle \int d(uv)=\int u\,dv+\int v\,du\]
Since \(\int d(uv)=uv\), solve for the integral we want.
\[\displaystyle \int u\,dv=uv-\int v\,du\]

DERIVATION
CHOOSE\(u=x\)
CHOOSE\(dv=e^{2x}\,dx\)
DIFFERENTIATE\(du=dx\)
INTEGRATE\(v=\frac12e^{2x}\)
\[\displaystyle \int xe^{2x}dx=x\cdot\frac12e^{2x}-\int\frac12e^{2x}\,dx\]
\[\displaystyle =\frac12xe^{2x}-\frac14e^{2x}+C\]

SOLUTION & VIDEO

SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 02
DERIVATION
CHOOSE\(u=\ln x\)
CHOOSE\(dv=x^2\,dx\)
DIFFERENTIATE\(du=\frac1x\,dx\)
INTEGRATE\(v=\frac13x^3\)
\[\displaystyle \int x^2\ln x\,dx=\frac13x^3\ln x-\int\frac13x^3\cdot\frac1x\,dx\]
\[\displaystyle =\frac13x^3\ln x-\frac13\int x^2\,dx=\frac13x^3\ln x-\frac19x^3+C\]
03

Hidden Product

PROBLEM
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int\ln x\,dx\).
SOLUTION & VIDEO
● REC EXPAND
▶
SOLUTION VIDEO // EX 03
DERIVATION
Rewrite as a product: \(\displaystyle \int\ln x\,dx=\int(\ln x)(1)\,dx\).
CHOOSE\(u=\ln x\)
CHOOSE\(dv=dx\)
DIFFERENTIATE\(du=\frac1x\,dx\)
INTEGRATE\(v=x\)
\[\displaystyle \int\ln x\,dx=x\ln x-\int x\cdot\frac1x\,dx=x\ln x-x+C\]

DERIVATION
Use \(u=x,\ dv=e^{2x}dx,\ du=dx,\ v=\frac12e^{2x}\).
\[\displaystyle \int_0^1xe^{2x}dx =\left[\frac12xe^{2x}\right]_0^1 -\frac12\int_0^1e^{2x}dx \]
\[\displaystyle =\left[\frac12xe^{2x}-\frac14e^{2x}\right]_0^1 =\left(\frac12e^2-\frac14e^2\right)-\left(0-\frac14\right) =\frac{e^2+1}{4} \]
