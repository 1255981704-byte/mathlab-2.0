LONG DIVISION  •  COMPLETING THE SQUARE
REWRITE FIRST; INTEGRATE SECOND

Inspect Before Integrating

STEP 1 — COMPARE DEGREES
IMPROPER RATIONAL FUNCTION
\[\deg P\ge\deg Q\Rightarrow\text{long divide first}\]

Rewrite the rational function as a polynomial plus a proper rational remainder.

STEP 2 — INSPECT THE QUADRATIC
If the denominator is an irreducible quadratic, complete the square.
\[x^2+bx+c=\left(x+\frac b2\right)^2+c-\frac{b^2}{4}\]
Then match an arctangent or logarithmic form.

Make the Fraction Proper

PROBLEM & SOLUTION
INTEGRAL
\[\int\frac{x^3+2x^2+x+1}{x^2+1}dx\]
WORKED SOLUTION
\[\frac{x^3+2x^2+x+1}{x^2+1}=x+2-\frac1{x^2+1}\]
\[\boxed{\frac{x^2}{2}+2x-\arctan x+C}\]
Never integrate numerator and denominator separately.

Complete the Square

POSITIVE SQUARE
ARCTANGENT
\[\int\frac{du}{u^2+a^2}=\frac1a\arctan\left(\frac ua\right)+C\]
DIFFERENCE OF SQUARES
LOGARITHM
\[\int\frac{du}{u^2-a^2}=\frac1{2a}\ln\left|\frac{u-a}{u+a}\right|+C\]

Absolute values are required.

Quadratic Denominator

PROBLEM & SOLUTION
INTEGRAL
\[\int\frac{dx}{x^2+6x+13}\]
WORKED SOLUTION
\[x^2+6x+13=(x+3)^2+2^2\]
\[\int\frac{dx}{(x+3)^2+2^2}=\boxed{\frac12\arctan\left(\frac{x+3}{2}\right)+C}\]
Differentiate to verify the one-half scale factor.

Linear Numerator over Quadratic

PROBLEM & SOLUTION
INTEGRAL
\[\int\frac{x+1}{x^2+4x+8}dx\]
WORKED SOLUTION
\[x+1=\frac12(2x+4)-1,\qquad x^2+4x+8=(x+2)^2+2^2\]
\[\int\frac{x+1}{x^2+4x+8}dx=\frac12\ln(x^2+4x+8)-\int\frac{dx}{(x+2)^2+2^2}\]
\[\boxed{\frac12\ln(x^2+4x+8)-\frac12\arctan\left(\frac{x+2}{2}\right)+C}\]

Shifted Quadratic Denominator

PROBLEM & SOLUTION
INTEGRAL
\[\int\frac{dx}{x^2-4x+3}\]
WORKED SOLUTION
\[x^2-4x+3=(x-2)^2-1=(x-1)(x-3)\]
\[\boxed{\frac12\ln\left|\frac{x-3}{x-1}\right|+C}\]
The completed-square and factored forms lead to equivalent logarithmic antiderivatives.

Strategy Map

ORDER OF OPERATIONS
1. Compare degrees; long divide if improper.
2. Simplify the proper remainder.
3. Complete the square for an irreducible quadratic.
4. Match the positive-square or difference-of-squares form.
5. Verify by differentiation.
COMMON ERRORS
Wrong completing-square constant.
Missing the scale factor in arctangent.
Missing absolute values in logarithms.
