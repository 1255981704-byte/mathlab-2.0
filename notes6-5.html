DATA LOG // SECTION 6.5

Long Division and Completing the Square

1. Rational Integrands

A rational function is a quotient of polynomials,

\[ \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}. \]

Before integrating, compare the degrees and inspect the denominator.

DECISION PROCESS
  1. If \(\deg P\geq\deg Q\), perform polynomial long division first.
  2. If the denominator contains an irreducible quadratic, complete the square.
  3. Rewrite the remaining proper rational expression into a standard logarithmic or inverse-trigonometric form.

2. Polynomial Long Division

Long division rewrites an improper rational function as

\[ \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=S(x)+\frac{R(x)}{Q(x)}, \qquad \deg R<\deg Q. \]
EXAMPLE 1: DIVIDE BEFORE INTEGRATING

Evaluate \[ \int\frac{x^3+2x^2+x+1}{x^2+1}\,dx. \]

Polynomial division gives \[ \frac{x^3+2x^2+x+1}{x^2+1} =x+2-\frac1{x^2+1}. \]

Therefore,

\[ \begin{aligned} \int\frac{x^3+2x^2+x+1}{x^2+1}\,dx &=\int\left(x+2-\frac1{x^2+1}\right)dx\\[3pt] &=\boxed{\frac{x^2}{2}+2x-\arctan x+C}. \end{aligned} \]
EXAMPLE 2: A LINEAR DIVISOR

Evaluate \[ \int\frac{x^2+1}{x-1}\,dx. \]

Since \[ \frac{x^2+1}{x-1}=x+1+\frac2{x-1}, \]

we obtain

\[ \begin{aligned} \int\frac{x^2+1}{x-1}\,dx &=\int\left(x+1+\frac2{x-1}\right)dx\\[3pt] &=\boxed{\frac{x^2}{2}+x+2\ln|x-1|+C}. \end{aligned} \]
WARNING // DO NOT INTEGRATE NUMERATOR AND DENOMINATOR SEPARATELY

In general,

\[ \int\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}\,dx \neq \frac{\int P(x)\,dx}{\int Q(x)\,dx}. \]

The quotient must first be simplified by algebra, substitution, completing the square, or partial fractions.

3. Completing the Square

For a quadratic expression,

\[ x^2+bx+c =\left(x+\frac b2\right)^2+c-\frac{b^2}{4}. \]

This reveals a shifted version of a standard inverse-trigonometric or logarithmic form.

TWO STANDARD QUADRATIC FORMS

For \(a>0\),

\[ \boxed{\int\frac{du}{u^2+a^2}=\frac1a\arctan\left(\frac ua\right)+C} \]

and

\[ \boxed{\int\frac{du}{u^2-a^2} =\frac1{2a}\ln\left|\frac{u-a}{u+a}\right|+C}. \]

4. Positive Completed Square

EXAMPLE 3: AN ARCTANGENT FORM

Evaluate \[ \int\frac{dx}{x^2+6x+13}. \]

Complete the square: \[ x^2+6x+13=(x+3)^2+4. \]

Let \(u=x+3\). Then

\[ \int\frac{dx}{(x+3)^2+2^2} =\frac12\arctan\left(\frac{x+3}{2}\right)+C. \]

Thus,

\[ \boxed{\int\frac{dx}{x^2+6x+13} =\frac12\arctan\left(\frac{x+3}{2}\right)+C}. \]
EXAMPLE 4: SEPARATE A DERIVATIVE TERM FIRST

Evaluate \[ \int\frac{x+1}{x^2+4x+8}\,dx. \]

The denominator has derivative \(2x+4\). Rewrite the numerator as

\[ x+1=\frac12(2x+4)-1. \]

Then

\[ \begin{aligned} \int\frac{x+1}{x^2+4x+8}\,dx &=\frac12\int\frac{2x+4}{x^2+4x+8}\,dx -\int\frac{dx}{(x+2)^2+4}\\[3pt] &=\boxed{\frac12\ln(x^2+4x+8) -\frac12\arctan\left(\frac{x+2}{2}\right)+C}. \end{aligned} \]

5. Difference of Squares After Completing

EXAMPLE 5: A LOGARITHMIC FORM

Evaluate \[ \int\frac{dx}{x^2-4x+3}. \]

Complete the square: \[ x^2-4x+3=(x-2)^2-1. \]

Let \(u=x-2\). Using the standard formula,

\[ \begin{aligned} \int\frac{du}{u^2-1} &=\frac12\ln\left|\frac{u-1}{u+1}\right|+C\\[3pt] &=\boxed{\frac12\ln\left|\frac{x-3}{x-1}\right|+C}. \end{aligned} \]

The denominator in this example also factors as \((x-1)(x-3)\). Partial fractions provide an equivalent method and lead to the same logarithmic family.

6. Verification and Common Errors

Differentiate the answer to Example 3. Let \(z=(x+3)/2\):

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac12\arctan z\right] &=\frac12\cdot\frac{1}{1+z^2}\cdot\frac12\\[3pt] &=\frac{1}{(x+3)^2+4} =\frac{1}{x^2+6x+13}. \end{aligned} \]
WARNING // COMMON ERRORS
  • Skipping long division when the rational function is improper.
  • Completing the square with the wrong constant.
  • Forgetting the scale factor \(1/a\) in the arctangent formula.
  • Omitting absolute values in logarithmic answers.
  • Failing to split the numerator into a denominator-derivative part and a remainder.

7. Principal Results

STRATEGY SUMMARY
\[ \boxed{\deg P\geq\deg Q\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\text{long divide first}} \] \[ \boxed{x^2+bx+c=\left(x+\frac b2\right)^2+c-\frac{b^2}{4}} \]

After completing the square, match the denominator to \(u^2+a^2\) or \(u^2-a^2\), and verify the result by differentiation.
