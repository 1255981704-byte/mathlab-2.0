Antiderivatives

STATEMENT & EXAMPLE

Antidifferentiation is the inverse process of differentiation. A function \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) on an interval \(I\) if

DEFINITION — ANTIDERIVATIVE
\[\displaystyle F'(x)=f(x)\quad\text{for every }x\in I.\]

A proposed antiderivative is verified by differentiation.

EXAMPLE
Differentiate the proposed function:
\[\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{x^2}{2}\right)=x.\]
Therefore, \(\frac{x^2}{2}\) is an antiderivative of \(f(x)=x\).

The General Antiderivative

STATEMENT & EXPLORATION

Adding a constant does not change a derivative. If \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) on an interval \(I\), every antiderivative has the form

GENERAL ANTIDERIVATIVE THEOREM
\[\displaystyle \boxed{F(x)+C}\]

The arbitrary constant \(C\) is the constant of integration.

The interval condition ensures that any two antiderivatives differ by a constant.

ONE DERIVATIVE — A FAMILY
For every constant \(C\),
\[\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{x^2}{2}+C\right)=x.\]
Thus the general antiderivative of \(x\) is \(\frac{x^2}{2}+C\).

Indefinite-Integral Notation

NOTATION & EXAMPLES

The family of all antiderivatives of a function is represented by an indefinite integral.

IF F′(x) = f(x)
\[\displaystyle \boxed{\int f(x)\,dx=F(x)+C}\]

\(\int\) is the integral sign, \(f(x)\) is the integrand, \(dx\) specifies the variable of integration.

An indefinite integral is a family of functions, not a single function.

QUICK EXAMPLES
\[\displaystyle \int -2\,dx=-2x+C\]
\[\displaystyle \int\cos x\,dx=\sin x+C\]
\[\displaystyle \int\sin x\,dx=-\cos x+C\]

Basic Integration Formulas

FORMULAS & APPLICATION
CONSTANT RULE
\[\displaystyle \int a\,dx=ax+C\]
POWER RULE — n ≠ −1
\[\displaystyle \int x^n\,dx=\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C\]
LOGARITHMIC FORMULA
\[\displaystyle \int\frac1x\,dx=\ln|x|+C\]

The power rule excludes \(n=-1\), because \(1/(n+1)\) would be undefined.

EXAMPLE — INTEGRATE x⁵
Increase the exponent by one, then divide by the new exponent:
\[\displaystyle \int x^5\,dx=\frac{x^6}{6}+C.\]
Check: \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^6/6+C)=x^5\).

Linearity of Indefinite Integrals

STATEMENT & WARNING

Sums and differences may be integrated term by term, and constant factors may be placed outside the integral.

SUM & DIFFERENCE RULES
\[\displaystyle \int(f\pm g)\,dx=\int f\,dx\pm\int g\,dx\]
CONSTANT-MULTIPLE RULE
\[\displaystyle \int cf(x)\,dx=c\int f(x)\,dx\]
PRODUCTS OF FUNCTIONS
Linearity does not apply to products. In general,
\[\displaystyle \int f(x)g(x)\,dx\ne\left(\int f(x)\,dx\right)\left(\int g(x)\,dx\right).\]
Integration has no product rule of this form.

Antiderivatives of Linear Inner Functions

PROBLEM & SOLUTION
Evaluate
INTEGRAL
\[\displaystyle \int\sin(2x)\,dx.\]

The chain rule introduces the derivative of an inner function:

CHAIN RULE
\[\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\cos(2x)=-2\sin(2x).\]

Reverse the chain rule by compensating for the factor 2.

WORKED SOLUTION
\[\displaystyle \boxed{\int\sin(2x)\,dx=-\frac12\cos(2x)+C}\]
More generally, for \(a\ne0\):
\[\displaystyle \int\sin(ax)dx=-\frac1a\cos(ax)+C\]

Principal Results

FORMULA SUMMARY
FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS
\[\displaystyle \begin{aligned} \int a\,dx&=ax+C,\\ \int x^n\,dx&=\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C &&(n\ne-1),\\ \int\frac1x\,dx&=\ln|x|+C,\\ \int\cos x\,dx&=\sin x+C,\\ \int\sin x\,dx&=-\cos x+C. \end{aligned}\]
FINAL CONNECTION
An antiderivative is one function \(F\) satisfying \(F'=f\).
\[\displaystyle \int f(x)\,dx=F(x)+C\]
Every indefinite integral can be verified by differentiating the result.
